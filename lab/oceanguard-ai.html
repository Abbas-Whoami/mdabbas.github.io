OceanGuard AI

An edge AI safety system for fishing boats. Tracks position continuously against pre-loaded maritime boundaries, alerts fishermen in their own language before they cross into restricted zones, and enables emergency SOS communication when needed — all running offline at sea.

"Protecting fishermen. Preserving borders. Saving lives."

The problem

Fishermen cross invisible borders every day — without knowing it

Hundreds of Indian fishermen are arrested every year by Sri Lankan, Pakistani, and Bangladeshi navies for crossing maritime borders. Their boats are seized — vessels worth ten to fifty lakh rupees that represent an entire family's livelihood. Many fishermen are detained for months. Some never return.

The sea has no visible boundary markers. Fishermen navigate by experience and intuition, but ocean currents and wind drift boats across borders unknowingly. Most boats carry no GPS, no alerts, and no communication technology. Government-issued GPS devices exist but display coordinates that fishermen cannot interpret. The result: a preventable crisis that repeats year after year at enormous human and economic cost.

The solution

A smart guardian on every boat — offline, in their language

01 Track

GPS continuously tracks the boat's position. The device comes pre-loaded with all maritime boundary data — international borders, state waters, protected marine reserves, seasonal ban zones.

02 Warn

As the boat approaches a restricted zone, a voice alert fires in the fisherman's local language — Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, or Konkani. Yellow, orange, and red warnings give time to turn back safely.

03 Monitor

The AI camera logs fishing activity and location compliance throughout the trip. Data syncs to a harbour management system when the boat returns to port.

04 SOS

In emergencies — medical, mechanical, or weather — the device sends an SOS with precise GPS coordinates to the coast guard and registered family contacts via satellite relay.

Key capabilities

What the product will do

Fully offline operation

All boundary data, zone maps, and AI models run on-device. No mobile signal or internet connection needed at sea — works in the most remote waters.

Local language voice alerts

Warnings are spoken in Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Konkani — in plain, clear language that any fisherman can understand immediately.

Boundary intelligence

Pre-loaded with international maritime borders, state jurisdiction lines, marine protected areas, and seasonal fishing ban zones — updated via harbour sync.

SOS and emergency relay

Satellite-assisted SOS sends GPS position to coast guard and family when internet or mobile is unavailable. One-button activation designed for stress situations.

Compliance logging

The system logs the full trip route, time in each zone, and any boundary proximity events — creating a verifiable compliance record for fisheries authorities.

Harbour sync and fleet view

When boats dock, trip data syncs to a state or co-operative fleet dashboard — giving port authorities, fisheries departments, and NGOs visibility across the entire fleet.

Who it is for

Target users

The direct users are India's coastal fishing communities — particularly in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and Gujarat, which account for the majority of arrested fishermen cases. The system would be deployed through state fisheries departments and fishing co-operatives who already have infrastructure for boat registration and equipment distribution. Central subsidies for maritime safety gear make government procurement the most viable go-to-market path, alongside NGOs already working on fishermen's rights and safety.
