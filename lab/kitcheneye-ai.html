KitchenEye AI

A 24/7 AI hygiene inspector for commercial kitchens. Connects to existing CCTV, detects violations in real time, scores daily compliance, and generates audit reports for FSSAI inspections — all processing happening on-device with no cloud dependency.

"Always watching. Always safe."

The problem

Kitchen hygiene fails silently, then catastrophically

India reports over 100 million cases of food poisoning annually, and unhygienic commercial kitchens are the primary cause. FSSAI regulations exist in theory but inspections happen once a year — if at all. Kitchens clean up for the inspector and immediately revert to their normal habits.

Most restaurants and cloud kitchens have CCTV footage sitting on hard drives that nobody watches. Violations happen every hour — staff handling food without gloves, uncovered ingredients, improper storage, skipped handwashing — but there is no system to catch them in real time. The cost of one viral video or outbreak incident is far higher than any investment in prevention.

The solution

Real-time detection, instant alerts, continuous compliance

01 Watch

The Jetson device connects to existing kitchen CCTV. AI monitors every corner of the kitchen 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — no new cameras needed.

02 Detect

The system recognises violations the moment they happen — no gloves, no hairnet, food left uncovered, spills, pests, improper storage, skipped handwashing steps.

03 Alert

Instant push notification to the kitchen manager. The violation is logged with a screenshot timestamp before staff can correct it and deny it happened.

04 Score and Report

Daily hygiene score with breakdown by violation type. Weekly compliance report. Full audit trail exportable for FSSAI inspections — show compliance, not just a clean kitchen on inspection day.

What it detects

Violations the system will flag

No gloves

Staff handling food without protective gloves — detected per individual, per station.

No hairnet or cap

Hair not covered during food preparation. Flags both kitchen staff and supervisors in the zone.

Uncovered food

Food left open and exposed on counters, prep surfaces, or storage areas — a primary contamination risk.

Skipped handwashing

Staff entering the kitchen or transitioning between tasks without the correct handwashing sequence.

Pest detection

Cockroaches, rodents, and flies detected near preparation or storage areas — alerts trigger immediately.

Floor hazards and spills

Wet floors, debris, and spills flagged both as hygiene risks and as slip-and-fall safety hazards.

Who it is for

Target users

The primary market is cloud kitchens, QSR chains, and hotel food and beverage operations in India — businesses that serve high volumes of food daily and carry significant brand risk from a single hygiene incident. Food aggregator platforms like Swiggy and Zomato are a natural distribution channel for mandating KitchenEye across their restaurant partners. Food processing units and catering companies are secondary markets with similar compliance needs.
