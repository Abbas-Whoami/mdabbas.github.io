In Planning · AR / Computer Vision

Magic Mirror

An AR-powered virtual try-on system for clothing retail and e-commerce. Walk up, stand in front of the display, and see yourself wearing any outfit in the store — instantly, with no changing room, no queue, and no guesswork about fit.

"Revolutionising how the world tries on clothes."

The problem

Trying on clothes is broken — in stores and online

The average shopper spends 25 to 40 minutes waiting in trial room queues. When they finally get in, the process of undressing, redressing, and repeating across multiple items is exhausting enough to make people give up and buy less — or walk out without buying at all. Retailers lose sales they should have closed.

Online is even worse. Without the ability to visualise fit, 30 to 40 percent of clothing purchases are returned. Each return costs the retailer in logistics, processing, and restocking — while the customer gets a frustrating wait for a refund. The problem is not the buying decision, it is the inability to see what the item will actually look like on.

The solution

See yourself in any outfit, instantly

01 Stand

The customer stands in front of a large display screen fitted with a depth camera. The system maps their body in real time — full dimensions, posture, proportions.

02 Browse

A touch panel or motion gesture scrolls through the store catalogue. Select any item and it appears on the person's reflection immediately.

03 Try

The AR overlay adapts the garment to the person's actual body shape, draping and scaling realistically. Move and the outfit moves with you.

04 Decide

The customer sees exactly how the item fits before committing. Save a look, share it, or proceed to purchase — no changing room needed.

Key capabilities

What the product will do

Real-time body mapping

Depth cameras capture a precise 3D model of the customer's body, accounting for height, width, and posture — updated live as they move.

Garment draping simulation

Physics-based rendering makes fabric fall and drape naturally — different materials behave differently, so cotton looks like cotton and silk looks like silk.

Full store catalogue integration

Syncs directly with the store's inventory system. All available sizes, colours, and variants are browsable and tryable immediately.

Multi-item outfit building

Layer tops, bottoms, outerwear, and accessories together to build complete looks before deciding what to purchase.

E-commerce mode

A lighter phone-camera version for online retail — upload a photo and see any product from the catalogue overlaid on your image before adding to cart.

Analytics dashboard

Retailers see which items are tried most, which lead to purchases, and which are consistently rejected — actionable insight for buying and merchandising teams.

Who it is for

Target users

The in-store product targets fashion retail chains and premium apparel stores that want to improve conversion, reduce fitting room throughput pressure, and offer a differentiated in-store experience. The e-commerce module targets online fashion platforms looking to reduce return rates and increase buyer confidence at the point of purchase. The two modes are designed to work together — a customer can use the in-store mirror, save their look, and return to the e-commerce extension at home.
