FurnCraft AI

Snap a photo of any furniture piece, let AI split it into editable components, customize every part, and export a production-ready design to the factory. The missing layer between customers and manufacturers.

"Canva for furniture — no CAD skills needed."

The problem

Custom furniture manufacturing is broken

A customer describes a design to a showroom manager. The manager interprets it and passes instructions to the factory. The factory builds something different. Everyone loses time, material, and money — and the customer is disappointed. This chain of miscommunication repeats every single day across thousands of custom furniture manufacturers.

Existing design tools like CAD and SolidWorks are built for engineers, not for showroom staff or customers. They require months of training, and they produce technical outputs that mean nothing to the person who just wants to see how their living room will look. The result is a massive gap between what was imagined and what gets built.

The solution

Snap. Split. Customize. Manufacture.

01 Capture

Take a photo of any furniture piece or upload an image. The AI instantly identifies the item type — sofa, chair, bed, cabinet, table.

02 AI Splits

The system breaks the furniture into individual editable components — legs, armrests, backrest, cushions, handles, fabric layers — each independently adjustable.

03 Customize

Mix parts from different pieces. Change colors, materials, and shapes in real time. Pull legs from Chair A, fabric from Sofa B, handles from Cabinet C.

04 Export

Generate a structured design spec — dimensions, materials, component list — ready for factory production with no ambiguity.

Key features

What the product will do

AI image recognition

Automatically identifies furniture type and detects individual components from a single photo — no manual labelling.

Smart component splitting

Separates handles, legs, backrest, seat, and armrests into independent editable layers — all from one source image.

Template library

Hundreds of pre-built component designs — handles, legs, fabrics, colors, materials — fully browsable and swappable.

Cross-furniture mixing

Combine parts freely across any furniture type. The system handles proportional scaling and visual rendering automatically.

Material catalogue

Teak, oak, plywood, MDF, fabric, leather, steel, aluminum — full material library with realistic grain and texture previews.

Production export

Exports a structured bill of materials and design brief that factory teams can act on directly — no verbal interpretation.

Who it is for

Target users

The primary users are custom furniture manufacturers who build made-to-order pieces and need a way to bridge the design conversation with customers before production begins. Retail showroom staff are the second key user — they can sit with a customer, build a design on the spot, and send an approved spec to the factory before the customer leaves the store. Interior designers and B2B corporate furniture procurement teams are natural expansion targets once the core product is validated.
